Artificial Intelligence (AI)

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the branch of computer science that
focuses on building systems capable of performing tasks that normally
require human intelligence. These tasks include learning, reasoning,
problem-solving, perception, understanding language, and
decision-making.

Brief History

-   1950: Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test.
-   1956: The term “Artificial Intelligence” was introduced at the
    Dartmouth Conference.
-   1980s: Expert systems became popular.
-   2012: Deep learning achieved major breakthroughs in image
    recognition.
-   2017: The Transformer architecture revolutionized Natural Language
    Processing.
-   Today: Generative AI models can create text, images, audio, video,
    and code.

Types of AI

1.  Narrow AI (Weak AI)
2.  General AI (AGI)
3.  Super AI (Theoretical)

Major Fields of AI

-   Machine Learning (ML)
-   Deep Learning (DL)
-   Natural Language Processing (NLP)
-   Computer Vision
-   Robotics
-   Reinforcement Learning
-   Generative AI

AI Workflow

1.  Collect Data
2.  Preprocess Data
3.  Train a Model
4.  Evaluate Performance
5.  Deploy the Model
6.  Monitor and Improve

Applications

-   Healthcare
-   Finance
-   Education
-   Manufacturing
-   Transportation
-   Cybersecurity
-   Agriculture
-   Entertainment

Advantages

-   Automates repetitive tasks
-   Improves accuracy
-   Works continuously
-   Analyzes large datasets
-   Supports better decision-making

Challenges

-   Bias in data
-   Privacy concerns
-   High computational cost
-   Explainability
-   Ethical concerns

Future of AI

AI is expected to become more capable, personalized, and integrated into
daily life. Areas such as autonomous systems, AI agents, scientific
discovery, and human-AI collaboration are expected to grow rapidly.
